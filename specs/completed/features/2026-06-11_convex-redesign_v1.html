1. The thesis (three independent analyses converged)

OptionsOwl's edge is convex / fat-tailed: a minority of trades become large runners and pay for many small losers. But the architecture was built for high-win-rate scalping, so it fights the edge at every stage. Three analyses (2026-06-11) agree:

  • Runner-prediction (validated, overfit-checked): ≥100% runners ARE predictable at entry — OOS AUC 0.80, top-decile 2.2× base (big-runner AUC 0.87, 3.8×). Runners live in: low premium, low DTE (0DTE 40% vs 4DTE 4%), index names (IWM/QQQ/SPY), Fridays. Survives within DTE strata + in the tradeable ($1–3, spread≤20%) subset.
  • Fundamental-logic audit: sizing is anti-convex (5 down-haircuts, no up-path); exits decapitate the tail (scaleout, CALL profit ceilings); DCA adds to losers; "score" is incoherent.
  • External research: scaleout "almost always reduces net profit + shrinks the right tail"; can't-predict-losers is the EXPECTED convex signature (edge is in exits/sizing, not entry filters); anti-martingale (add to winners) > averaging down; keep breakeven ratchet + 0DTE risk controls.
The unified root cause: the system excludes its own runners. Gates BLOCK the cheap near-ATM 0DTE setups where runners live (real trades hit +100% only 3.8% vs 25% in the unfiltered runner population) → sizing CAPS what survives (the $442-of-$19K NVDA) → exits CUT the survivors (scaleout ⅓ at +20%, profit ceilings). Fix all three together or none works.

2. Redesign — entry / sizing / exit as ONE convex system

2.1 Entry — thin & permissive, let runner-setups through

  • Stop trying to filter losers (direction refuted, confidence-scaling overfit, and it's the wrong job for a convex strategy). Keep only the **real risk controls** (spread gate, EOD cutoff, position/portfolio caps, VIX, kill switch, daily-loss CB).
  • Relax the gates that block runner setups — premium cap + score floors currently exclude the cheap, low-DTE, near-ATM calls that are the runners. Admit high-P(runner) candidates that today get filtered.
  • Add a P(runner) score (the validated classifier) to the entry context — used for sizing (2.2/2.3), not as a hard reject.
  • Micro-bounce entry fill (validated, dipconfirm_tuning.md): enter on the first N=2 tick bounce off the local premium low (90s cap, always-fill fallback at market) on the live WS feed — catches the dip bottom: ~0.9% cheaper entries (~$25–30/trade), median entry at the in-window low vs +3.1% above today. Also fixes the as-coded bug where an ML-signal timeout records the unfillable low_water as the entry. In V7's 1-min backtest, model as an entry-fill improvement (enter near the intra-bar low / shave the validated ~0.9% off the ask). Free basis-points per entry — NOT a fader fix; real validation is the live-tick paper test behind ENABLE_DIP_CONFIRM_MICROBOUNCE.

2.2 Sizing — convex, up-tilted to P(runner), anti-martingale

  • Remove the anti-convex haircuts: kill the backwards 60% confidence tier (refuted OOS); rethink slot-dilution (÷MAX_CONCURRENT under-deploys when few signals fire); keep MAX_POSITION_PCT + portfolio risk as the hard ceiling.
  • Tilt budget to P(runner): 1.5–2× for top-decile (46–55% runner rate, ~268% avg peak), flat middle, 0.5×/skip bottom-30% (<7%). Risk-bounded by the position cap + V5 stops.
  • Flip DCA → anti-martingale: ADD to confirmed winners (one capped add, whole stack under one trail), NOT average down into dips (current V6 DCA is backwards).

2.3 Exit — cut the body fast, let the tail run uncapped

  • Remove the right-tail caps: no scaleout (sells ⅓ at +20% — the #1 leak per both audit + research) and NO CALL profit ceiling — propagate the PUT lesson (no-ceiling went +$20K→+$63K).
  • Widening trail on confirmed runners (give the tail room), vs current ~37% giveback.
  • Keep the breakeven ratchet (convex-positive, truncates only the left tail) and a fast stall-stop to cut the body (faders) quickly — small initial bet + fast stall-cut keeps losers small.

3. Why this also satisfies "stop losing money"

Small flat base bet → losers stay small (we add size only to trades already working). Up-size + uncapped exit → runners get big. That's the asymmetry: lots of small −X, occasional large +Y. It does NOT require predicting losers (we can't) — only sizing/holding the runners we CAN identify.

4. Validation protocol (mandatory — this is a real-money entry+sizing+exit overhaul)

  1. Full-chain PAPER test FIRST (the runner-prediction agent's required next step): relaxed gates for high-P(runner) + P(runner)-tilted sizing + uncapped/widening exit, run in paper/shadow on live data. Sizing-up does nothing unless the runner setups are first allowed through the gates — so test the chain end-to-end, not pieces.
  2. Backtest each component + the whole on the (backfilling) multi-year data: scaleout OFF vs ON (P95/P99 tail capture, not just net P&L); CALL no-ceiling; P(runner)-tilted sizing; multi-window walk-forward + OOS. Reject in-sample-only wins (the confidence-scaling discipline).
  3. Phase the live rollout: exit changes first (no scaleout/ceiling — exit-side, doesn't raise entry risk), then gate-relaxation+sizing together (the part that increases $/trade) only after paper + win-rate hold. Keep .env rollback + daily-loss CB.
