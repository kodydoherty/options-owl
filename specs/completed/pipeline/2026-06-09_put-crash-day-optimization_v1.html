1. Overview

On 2026-06-09, the market dropped ~2.7% (SPY) to ~12.9% (AMD). Our bot entered ZERO PUT trades despite massive downside moves. Investigation revealed three root causes: the PUT entry timing model blocked 100% of signals, two net-loser tickers drained PUT profits, and the backtest had a critical ATM strike selection bug that hid the real opportunity.

Backtested result with all fixes (60 days, CALL+PUT combined):
$+188,250 total — CALLs $+88K (177 trades, 77% WR) + PUTs $+100K (273 trades, 73% WR)
Profit Factor 2.07, Sharpe 12.08, Max Drawdown 9.6%

2. Problem Statement

On the biggest crash day in months (6/9/26), the bot missed $6,400+ in PUT profit because:

  1. PUT entry timing model blocked 168/178 pattern passes — trained on normal markets, confidence drops to 0.31-0.45 during crashes. AUC 0.94 but net destructive ($53K opportunity cost vs $12K saved).
  2. AMZN and GOOGL are net PUT losers — -$10K and -$7K respectively over 60 days.
  3. Backtest ATM strike selection bug — used bottom-of-day price to pick strike (e.g., SPY $734 instead of $744), causing OTM gate to block everything. Backtest-only fix.

3. Goals & Success Criteria

  • Capture PUT opportunities on crash days (6/9 target: $5K+ vs $0 actual)
  • Eliminate net-loser PUT tickers without affecting CALL performance
  • Maintain or improve overall Profit Factor (≥2.0) and Win Rate (≥73%)
  • No degradation to CALL P&L ($88K+ over 60 days)
  • All changes deployable before market open 6/10

6. Backtest-Only Fixes (for accurate future backtesting)

6.1 ATM PUT Strike Selection

Bug: ORDER BY MIN(ABS(og.underlying_price - oohlc.strike)) picked the strike closest to the underlying at any point during the day. On crash days, this selected the bottom-of-day strike (e.g., SPY $734) which was deep OTM at market open.

Fix: Use the opening underlying price to select the ATM PUT strike. This matches production behavior where the bot resolves ATM at scan time (near open).

Impact: SPY 6/9 went from $0 to $+4,218. Total 6/9 PUT P&L: $+73 → $+6,463.

6.2 Gate Block Tracking

5 gates were incrementing signals_gate_blocked without adding to the gate_blocks dict, making debugging impossible:

  • min_premium — premium below $0.20 floor
  • premium_cap — premium above $6 cap
  • spread_gate — bid-ask spread > 15%
  • same_direction — too many same-direction positions
  • gfv_limit — good faith violation buying power limit

6.3 ThetaData Download (from earlier session)

  • Fixed find_atm_strike() to use open price instead of close price
  • Added --otm-below flag for asymmetric PUT depth (e.g., --otm 4 --otm-below 10)
  • Default OTM depth changed from 2 to 4

9. Risks & Rollback

  • More PUT trades = more exposure: Disabling entry timing increases PUT volume from ~13 to ~273 trades/60 days. Mitigated by PUT_SCALP_CONFIG hard stop (-50%) and breakeven ratchet (+20% → floor = entry).
  • AMZN/GOOGL exclusion may miss occasional winners: Low risk — both are net losers over 60 days. Can re-evaluate after 30 more trading days.

Rollback Plan

Set PUT_ENTRY_TIMING_THRESHOLD=0.85 in docker-compose.yml and docker compose up -d owlet-kody. Remove AMZN,GOOGL from PUT_EXCLUDED_TICKERS in settings.py and rebuild.

10. Future Optimizations (deferred)

  • Bear-mode premium cap relaxation: Raise PUT premium cap from $6 to $12 when SPY down >0.5%. Only 32 bear-mode trades in backtest — needs more data before production.
  • Bear-mode OTM distance relaxation: Widen OTM gate 2x during crash. Needs validation that it doesn't increase losses on mild red days.
  • Multi-strike PUT scanning: After a PUT trade closes, re-enter same ticker on the new ATM strike. Tested in backtest but picked suboptimal strikes mid-day. Needs smarter strike selection logic (e.g., only re-enter if underlying moved >1% since last entry).
  • Afternoon danger gate for PUTs: Currently applies to PUTs in non-bear mode. Could remove entirely since PUTs scan all day (5-360min) in production.
