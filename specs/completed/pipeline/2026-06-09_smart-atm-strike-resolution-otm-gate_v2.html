Part A: OTM Entry Gate — V2 Complete

A.1 Problem

The ML scan pipeline resolved ATM strikes once per day. When the underlying moved, the bot traded stale OTM options that bled to zero. AMZN #249 on 2026-06-09 lost -$414 (-64.5%) because the $250 CALL was resolved when AMZN was ~$250 but entered when AMZN had dropped to $248.92.

A.2 Solution (Deployed)

  1. Dynamic ATM re-resolution: When underlying drifts >1% from strike resolution price, re-resolve from Redis and reset scan state (bot_runner.py)
  2. Per-ticker OTM gate (V2): Dollar-based thresholds using strike grid intervals from ThetaData research (6.7M rows, 14 tickers, 124 trading days)

Part B: PUT Entry Timing Gate — Blocking All PUTs on Crash Days

B.1 Problem (discovered 2026-06-09, live market crash)

During a major market selloff on 2026-06-09 (SPY down >1.5%, "$2 trillion erased"), the bot did not enter a single PUT trade despite the PUT pattern model consistently passing (conf 0.68–0.79 across SPY, AMZN, GOOGL, AAPL). SPY PUTs went up 680–730% that day.

Root cause: The PUT entry timing model (AUC 0.94, threshold 0.85) blocked every signal. SPY entry timing confidence was only 0.31–0.45, AMZN was 0.67–0.78 — all well below the 0.85 threshold. The model was trained on normal market conditions and doesn't generalize to crash days.

B.5 Analysis

Key finding: CRASH and DYNAMIC modes had zero impact during the 60-day backtest because the SPY -0.5%/30min trigger didn't activate on any of the 13 existing entry timing pass moments. The crash days in this window were gradual enough that SPY didn't drop 0.5% in any 30-minute window where a PUT pattern signal was already active. These strategies need a longer backtest window that includes true crash days (e.g., the April 2026 tariff selloff) to properly evaluate.
REGIME mode is the only smart strategy that produced different results. It added 44 trades (13→57), maintained 80.7% WR, and generated $+5,942 (+60% vs current). The trade-off: PF dropped from 5.47 to 1.70 and MaxDD increased from 3.1% to 10.8%. It was profitable but much less capital-efficient than the strict filter.
DISABLED is the P&L monster: $+57,356 (15x more than current) with 351 trades. The pattern model alone (AUC 0.80) finds plenty of profitable PUT opportunities. The entry timing model at 0.85 filters out 96% of them (351 → 13), keeping only the highest-confidence entries. On normal days this is fine, but on crash days it leaves massive profits on the table.

Part C: Conclusion & Decision

Verdict: PUT entry timing DISABLED. Pattern model is sufficient.

The PUT entry timing model (AUC 0.94) has an excellent ability to rank entry quality but is net destructive as a filter. It blocks $53K in profits to avoid $12K in losses over 60 days. On crash days (the primary PUT profit opportunity), it blocks 100% of signals with confidence 0.31–0.45.

Deployed 2026-06-09: PUT_ENTRY_TIMING_THRESHOLD=0.0 (disabled). The PUT pattern model (AUC 0.80, threshold 0.65) handles entry filtering. The V5 FSM exit engine (breakeven ratchet, adaptive trailing, hard stops) manages risk.

Key Lessons

  • PUT entry timing is net destructive (-$53K opportunity cost). Unlike CALL entry timing which improves quality, PUT entry timing removes 96% of profitable signals. The pattern model alone produces 74.6% WR on 351 trades — good enough with the exit engine managing risk.
  • ML models trained on normal markets fail on crash days. SPY PUT entry timing confidence was 0.31–0.45 on a real -1.5% crash day. Even lowering the threshold to 0.75 only catches AMZN. The model is fundamentally wrong about crash conditions.
  • PUTs need volume to be profitable. 13 trades at 92% WR = $3.7K. 351 trades at 75% WR = $57K. The extra losers are manageable with exit stops; the extra winners are transformative.
  • Breakeven ratchet is the real safety net. Once a PUT hits +20%, the floor is entry price. This means winners can't become losers, making the lower WR acceptable.
  • CRASH/DYNAMIC modes need longer backtests. 60-day window didn't contain sharp enough intraday SPY drops. Extend to 120+ days to include April tariff selloff.
